Journey of Integrating Nvidia's DDGI into Hazel Engine

Introduction

As a graphics programmer, I've always been fascinated by the potential of dynamic global illumination (GI) to bring a new level of realism to games. When Nvidia introduced their Dynamic Diffuse Global Illumination (DDGI) technology, I saw an opportunity to enhance the Hazel Engine with this cutting-edge feature.

Understanding DDGI

Before diving into the integration process, I spent a considerable amount of time understanding the principles behind DDGI. Nvidia's DDGI is a real-time ray-traced global illumination solution that approximates indirect diffuse lighting. It leverages the power of modern GPUs to calculate lighting conditions dynamically, which is a significant improvement over traditional static light baking methods.

Integration Process

The integration process began with setting up the necessary environment for DDGI within the Hazel Engine. This involved configuring the engine to support the required Vulkan Ray Tracing features and Nvidia's RTX technology.

Next, I integrated the DDGI shader provided by Nvidia into Hazel's rendering pipeline. This shader is responsible for calculating the indirect diffuse lighting based on the scene's geometry and materials.

One of the challenges I faced during this process was ensuring that the DDGI calculations were correctly incorporated into Hazel's existing lighting model. This required a deep understanding of both Hazel's rendering pipeline and the workings of the DDGI shader.

Results and Optimizations

After successful integration, the results were immediately noticeable. Scenes rendered in Hazel now had a new level of depth and realism thanks to the dynamic global illumination provided by DDGI.

However, the initial implementation was quite performance-intensive. To address this, I spent time profiling and optimizing the DDGI integration. This involved fine-tuning the DDGI settings, optimizing the ray-tracing pipeline, and implementing techniques such as spatial and temporal denoising to improve performance.

Conclusion

Integrating Nvidia's DDGI into the Hazel Engine was a challenging but rewarding experience. It not only improved the visual quality of the engine but also provided me with a deeper understanding of modern real-time rendering techniques. I'm excited to see what developers can create with this new feature in Hazel Engine.
