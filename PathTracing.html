Path Tracing

Path tracing is a powerful rendering technique that simulates the way light travels and interacts with objects in a scene. It's capable of producing highly realistic images by accurately modeling complex lighting effects such as reflections, refractions, and shadows.

Implementation

In my implementation of path tracing in the Hazel engine, I followed these steps:

  1. **Ray Generation**: For each pixel in the image, I generated a ray starting from the camera and passing through the pixel.
  2. **Ray Intersection**: I used a BVH (Bounding Volume Hierarchy) to efficiently find the closest object that each ray intersects.
  3. **Shading**: For each intersection, I calculated the color of the pixel based on the material of the object and the lighting in the scene.
  4. **Secondary Rays**: I generated secondary rays for reflections and refractions, and recursively repeated the process.
