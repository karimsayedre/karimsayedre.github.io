Experience

Alright, let's dive into my journey in the world of rendering engineering. It all started when I was working on my final year project which was a rendering engine. Since then, it's been all about pushing the boundaries of visual quality and performance.

As a final year project student, I built my own rendering engine called Beyond. My primary acheivements were simple and they included:

  • Developed a derferred lighting renderer to enhance performance.
  • Implemeted Screen-Space Ambient Occlusion (SSAO).
  • Implemented Point and spot lights with soft shadows.
My work on these features helped to enhance the engine's rendering capabilities.

As a volunteer rendering engineer for the Hazel Engine, I contributed to the development of a powerful 3D game engine. My primary responsibilities included:

  • Implementing advanced graphics techniques such as Ground Truth Ambient Occlusion (GTAO), Screen Space Reflections (SSR), and Horizon-Based Ambient Occlusion (HBAO).
  • Enhancing the engine's lighting techniques to improve the realism and visual appeal of the rendered scenes.
My work on these features helped to enhance the engine's rendering capabilities and improve the overall visual quality of games developed using Hazel.

During my military service year, I worked on Tensor, a propriatary fork of the Hazel Engine maintained by Tensor Foundries Inc. I was able to spend a week (holiday from military service) every 2 weeks spent at my militray unit. Despite the constraints, I was able to make substantial contributions to Tensor:

  • Implemented Linearly Transformed Cosines (LTC) Area Lights, a technique that enhances the realism of lighting in 3D scenes.
  • Integrated Weighted Average Order Independent Transparency (OIT), an advanced rendering technique that improves the visual quality of transparent objects.
  • Added Planar Reflections to the engine, a feature that significantly enhances the realism of reflective surfaces.
These features not only improved the visual quality and realism of the Tensor engine but also demonstrated my ability to work effectively under challenging conditions.

After completing my military service, I continued to apply and expand my skills in graphics programming by working on a forked version of the Hazel Engine. In this independent project, I have:

  • Integrated RTXGI and Path Tracing to improve the engine's real-time lighting capabilities.
  • Utilized the Vulkan Ray Tracing pipeline to introduce advanced ray tracing features into the engine.
These enhancements have further expanded the capabilities of the Hazel Engine, demonstrating my ability to independently drive the development and improvement of complex graphics engines.


Rendering Techniques

Hazel Engine is a simple yet powerful 3D game engine developed by Studio Cherno and volunteers. It's currently available for use and has big plans for the future. The engine is primarily an early-stage interactive application and rendering engine. It's being developed with the aim of creating a powerful 3D engine, but also to serve as an educational tool for teaching game engine design and architecture. The Hazel Engine was started by Yan Chernikov in late 2018, and it's now officially developed by Studio Cherno, a company started in 2021.
